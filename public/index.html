Education

Hello, world! My name is Alex and I'm from a small town in Wisconsin. A few states away in Washington State & at the University of Puget Sound is where I decided to pursue a bachelor of science in computer science.

My coursework has mostly consisted of computer science classes including introduction to computer science, data structures, discreet mathematics, computer architecture and assembly language, computer graphics, software engineering, network programming, principles of concurrent programming, database management systems, and operating systems.

Additional noteworthy coursework includes financial accounting, principles of financial management, international finance, and principles of graphic design.

I feel very fortunate to work for the Mathematics and Computer Science department at the University of Puget Sound as a Computer Science Teaching Assistant. My job responsibilities include tutoring undergraduate university students of all skill levels, and grading for the data structures class, CS261.

For the summer of 2017, I have taken a role as a Research Assistant for Mathematics and Computer Science Department at Univ. of Puget Sound. My research is an extension of Alexia Ingerson's (Intel) work under Professor David Chiu in the University of Puget Sound Computer Science Department.The project is open source and if you're interested in reviewing or contributing the source is here -> bit-map engine.

For the University of Puget Sound Association for Computing Machinery chapter, I have served as the chapter treasurer. My responsibilities included producing and adhering to an annual budget. They also included delivering club presentations and chapter meeting organization.

I'm very happy to say that I am the founder and president of the student run organization, The Puget Sound Podcast. The Puget Sound Podcast is an educationally themed podcast especially concerned with interviews, discussions, and debates that's recorded and produced by students at the University of Puget Sound. The audio recording are made available on the iTunes store, SoundCloud, Facebook, or on our website, pugetsoundpodcast.com

During my time working towards my under graduate degree, I took an internship as a logistics coordinator at the Northwest Outward Bound School. In addition to the logistics coordinator work experience, I am also Wilderness First Responder Certified (WFR), CPR, and AED through the Wilderness Medical Society.

For three years of my time pursuing an undergraduate degree, I've worked as a volunteer instructor and treasurer for the Puget Sound Yoga Club.

Another noteworthy accolade during undergrad was my time studying abroad in the Northeast Himalayas with a group of 6 students and two professors. The program consisted mostly of ethnographic studies.

Web Development

I am a web developer and artist and I learned the fundamentals of web development especially in my software engineering course at the University of Puget Sound. The unique approach I take to web development is influenced by my artistic creativity. It is important to note that I consider myself to be proficient in JavaScript, HTML, and CSS. Also noteworth, I have experience with full stack development including Mongo, Express, Angular, and Node; I am a back-end systems administrator -> I manage my web hosting on my own linux machines.
